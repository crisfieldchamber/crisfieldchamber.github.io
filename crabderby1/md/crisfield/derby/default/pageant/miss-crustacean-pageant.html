Miss Crustacean Pageant
Thursday, September 3, 2015

Crisfield High School

The Miss Crustacean Pageant is open to girls 15-18 who are residents of Somerset County. Coaching begins in early May. Girls who wish to compete should contact Kathy Crockett in the spring. Thirteen girls are competing in the pageant this year.

The girls meet for practice twice a week where they are coached in such things as how to walk and how to remove the sarong during the bathing suit competition. They also practice an opening number.

Enrollment in the competition closes once the girls are professionally photographed for the Miss Photogenic competition. The girls need two casual outfits for the photo shoot. Ten photographs are taken. Each girl chooses 4 from the 10 to be displayed on a poster for judging. The judges select Miss Photogenic from the poster board. The girls vote among themselves for Miss Congeniality.

In addition to the casual outfits, the girls need an approved swim suit, a costume for the opening number, business attire and an evening gown. Kathy Crockett has a closet full of pageant clothes and girls are welcome to "shop" there.

To compete, each girl must find a sponsor, usually a local business. A girl approaches a sponsor with an official pageant letter. The sponsor must give the girl $125 for her personal use. The money is for the girl, not the pageant. Typically it will go toward the costume for the opening number. Sponsors get two tickets to the pageant and a year of publicity if their girl wins.

Coaching ensures that the girls are well prepared for the competition. They hold mock interviews and practice answering "fish bowl" questions thought up by Kathy and her assistant Kim East. On the night of the pageant they are poised and confident.

The pageant begins with a private interview. Wearing business attire, the girls meet with the judges back stage. After the interview the girls perform the opening number for the audience. Then each girl introduces herself to the audience and thanks her sponsor.

Introductions are followed by the swim suit competition and the evening gown competition, during which emcee Adam Riggin does a voice over to tell the audience more about the girls. The voice over dialog is written by the girls and includes their accomplishments and future plans.

After the evening gown competition 5 finalists are selected. The finalists each answer a "fish bowl" question, a question on a slip of paper picked from a fish bowl.

Finally judging takes place. The first winner announced is Miss Congeniality. Then Miss Photogenic. The court is announced by 3rd place, 2nd place, 1st Runner Up and Miss Crustacean.
